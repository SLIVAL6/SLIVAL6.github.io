PACSTUDENT VS. THE MUTANT SLIME HORROR

Development Period August 2022 - February 2023
Project Links
Relevant Skills

PACSTUDENT VS. THE MUTANT SLIME HORROR (or just Pacstudent) is a spin on the classic arcade game Pac-Man. It is a project created for the university subject 31263 Introduction to Computer Game Development at UTS.

For this subject, students were required to completely recreate Pac‑Man from scratch, but with completely different visuals, audio and enemy behaviour from the original. The second level also includes a brand-new mechanic, where the map is completely covered in darkness, and the player has an area of vision around them that slowly decreases over time.

As this was a solo project, I was responsible for everything, including programming, design, art, et cetera. All art and animations were created by myself in Aseprite.

I decided to go for a simple colour palette of black, white and green in order to simplify the art and animation processes for myself. This simple colour scheme and art style was inspired by the games Downwell, Luftrausers, and Minit. All three of these games have a limited colour palette, and yet have visuals that look great and stand out, which is why I decided to go with this simple visual style for Pacstudent.

Since Pacstudent was created as an assessment task, there were a few requirements that students needed to follow. One of those was that all of the movements of the player and enemies needed to be implemented through linear lerping and tweening. All tweening code needed to be created from scratch as external tween libraries could not be used. Due to this implementation of movement, the controls may feel slightly unresponsive as the player needs to finishing lerping to a grid position before processing the last input.

Another assessment requirement was that each enemy needed to have its own unique AI behaviour. External libraries and Unity Pathfinding were not allowed, and all behaviours needed to be coded from scratch.

Each enemy has a unique behaviour:

  • Enemy 1: Move away from the player.
  • Enemy 2: Move closer to the player.
  • Enemy 3: Move in a random direction.
  • Enemy 4: Move clockwise around the perimeter of the map.

The second level features a new mechanic where the player has a limited area of vision that decreases over time. I decided to go with this design as I felt that it would fit the overall eerie theme. The mechanic would make it difficult for the player to keep track of where all the enemies are, and keep the player guessing. It would also lead to moments where the player would be surprised by an enemy turning a corner when they least expect it.

Ocassionally, a flask collectable will float across the screen in a straight line, and act as a source of light. The player can collect this to get bonus points and also increase their area of vision. This leads to gameplay where the player is encouraged to collect flasks or suffer the consequence of having severely limited vision.
